Reviews of June Events

Author: Jewel Gutierrez
Date: May 2025

Cusco is the most pupullar in the months of June and July here you can find my reviews towards the June events I went within last year hosted in Cusco City! Note that for all of these events it is recomended you stay aware of your surroundings as robberies can occur. With that said you can have a busy schedule filled as parades, the whole month enjoy seeing art, dances, large concert and firework show for free!

Corpus Christi

Overall Experience

Location: Plaza De Armas
Time: Afternoon
Warnings: High Traffic, Lack of restroom facilities, Crowdness

A religious event that consists of dedicated young and old peruvians to carry the saint statues from their home chourch. This yearly event has a lot of history that you can read more of here! -->

Pros: This event is really fun to witness as the poeple who carry these heavy statues are in charge of decorating each of the saints differently each year. The event itself last a total of days, where if you miss one day going somewhere you can witness their farewell, race, and dance events that happen. The plaza gets filled and if you want a close look of the statues you can either be near the path of where they carry the statues into the main church or inside the church itself where they will all be gathered. My mother said that they take them there and they would gossip to eachother.

Cons: Now because it is a lonng event during the afternoon good luck trying to find a taxi that will take you to the plaza or even try and get out of the plaza. Busses will be full, restaurants will be booked and hard to get a balcony view. It was alsodifficult to find bathrooms so be wary of how much liquid you intake and definetly go before leaving your place of stay. In this even you have to be very wary of your surroundings and wait to cross or move around when the statues are close to you. As there will be a lot of pushing and yelling and perhaps a whip being whiplashed on the floor if you do are too close to them.

Inti Raymi

Overall Experience

Location: Plaza De Armas
Time: Afternoon
Warnings: Extreme crowdness, High Traffic, You will get stuck

A beutuiiful recreation of Inan ritual where many of the cutys opoeple would participate, march, sing, and dance to Incan songs and wear vestiments.
CLick here to learn more!

Pros: This event is one of the most popular for locals and tourists for good reason as there is so many participants that show off the Inca sacred ritual for free! This is a public event that is highly recomended to see as every year it is a show that starts all the way from Saqsayhuaman to the Plaza. There is a show with music, dances, and a parade where they carry the Inca and the princesses. Even the govener had to attend as he will be talked with the Inca. It lasts for a good while and you can catch a glimpse if you didn;t have a good spot after the main speech is held and the people go off around the plaza dancing and the worriors march. There will be plenty of food vendors where you can grab a snack while you wait for the show.

Cons: While this is an important tradition and many people will participate in this recreation iut is very very important you get there early if you want a good spot. Since I live a little away from the plaza it was really hard to find a tazi that was willing to go through the traffic to get near the area. Streets will be closed and if you make it even an hour before you will have a hard time to get throughg the many people that have already settled with their kids, stools. So come early or you will miss out on a good vbiw I couldnt get good pictures. Another downside is that if you do not know quechua you will not understand a thinkg they say I was lucky to have been with my mother fot this as she was able to translate it for me. It is a language that does not have live translation taken in place so you will just have to admire how accurate the display is as they will talk in Quechua and sing in quechua as well.
